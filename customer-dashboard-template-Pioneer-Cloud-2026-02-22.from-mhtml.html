Readiness narrative

Measuring cyber readiness with Immersive

Immersive does not just train teams. It measures real-world capability through hands-on labs and realistic exercises, so you can see where readiness is strong, where gaps remain, and how quickly those gaps are closing.

For Pioneer Cloud, this means defensible evidence you can use with leadership and external stakeholders: clear baselines, visible movement over time, and proof aligned to board and regulatory expectations.

PROVE

Produce evidence of readiness under pressure

Run realistic simulations that mirror scenarios that can matter in investment firms and portfolio operations (ransomware, business email compromise, data exfiltration, third-party compromise). Capture performance signals across security, IT, leadership, and communications, and export an after-action report.

  • Pressure-test incident response, crisis leadership, and cross-team coordination
  • Validate playbooks across corporate teams and repeatable portfolio patterns
  • Generate post-exercise evidence that stands up to investor, auditor, and regulator scrutiny
IMPROVE

Improve skills where risk concentrates

Where gaps surface in exercises, use hands-on, role-specific labs for security teams, IT, developers, and high-impact business roles. Build repeatable practice for selected teams and, where relevant, portfolio environments.

  • Target upskilling for cloud, identity, phishing/BEC, AppSec, and IR workflows
  • Support secure engineering for fintech and data platforms
  • Track improvement trends by role, team, and programme over time
REPORT

Produce defensible evidence and reporting

If completion metrics are useful but insufficient for stakeholders, add scenario-based performance evidence (accuracy, speed, and decision quality) and export it in an executive-, investor-, and audit-ready format.

  • Stakeholder-ready dashboards and after-action reporting
  • Evidence that supports privacy, safeguarding, and operational resilience expectations
  • Clear prioritisation: what to fix, who needs support, and what improved
